Book I: The Fullness
Before beginnings, before stars, before darkness, before light, there was the One.
Not a god among gods.
Not a ruler seated upon a throne.
Not a creator fashioning worlds with hands.
The One was beyond all such things.
Invisible, immeasurable, unknowable.
No name could contain it, for names belong to things that can be distinguished from other things. The One had no opposite and no equal. It existed beyond being and non-being, beyond thought and silence, beyond all categories that would one day arise within creation.
The sages would later call this source the Monad, the Invisible Spirit, the Ineffable Father.
Yet even these names were only shadows.
The One simply was.
Within the infinite stillness of the One arose a movement瑈ot a movement through space, for space did not yet exist, nor a movement through time, for time had not yet begun.
It was self-knowing.
The Father gazed upon itself.
And from that perfect contemplation emerged the First Thought.
She appeared like a reflection upon a motionless sea.
She was Barbelo.
The Forethought.
The Womb of the All.
The First Power.
The Mother-Father.
She stood as the image of the Invisible Spirit, neither separate from the One nor identical to it, but the first revelation of its hidden nature.
Through Barbelo, the Father became knowable.
Through Barbelo, possibility entered existence.
The Invisible Spirit beheld Barbelo and rejoiced.
And together they emanated further powers, each unfolding from the perfection of those before them.
First came Foreknowledge.
Then Incorruptibility.
Then Eternal Life.
Then Truth.
Each emerged not through labor or command but through divine abundance, as light radiates from a flame without diminishing its source.
These powers formed the beginning of the Pleroma鍟he Fullness.
Realm upon realm of living light unfolded.
Aeons came into being, eternal beings paired in harmonious syzygies, masculine and feminine principles joined in perfect balance.
Each reflected a different aspect of the Infinite.
None ruled over another.
None desired dominion.
All existed in unity.
The Pleroma expanded not outward but inward, revealing ever greater depths of the Father's inexhaustible nature.
At the center of this divine order emerged the Autogenes.
The Self-Begotten One.
The Living Christ.
He was not born as mortals are born.
He arose directly from the union of the Invisible Spirit and Barbelo.
Within him existed the perfect pattern of all things that would ever be.
The Aeons beheld him and recognized him as the image of divine completeness.
Around him gathered countless luminous beings.
Angels of incorruptible light.
Living thoughts.
Radiant intelligences.
Each participating in the harmony of the Fullness.
No suffering existed there.
No ignorance.
No separation.
No death.
For death requires division, and division had not yet entered reality.
The Pleroma existed as a single living song.
Yet at the furthest boundary of the Fullness dwelt one whose beauty rivaled the stars themselves.
Her name was Sophia.
Wisdom.
Youngest among the Aeons.
Brilliant beyond measure.
Within Sophia burned a longing unlike any other.
The other Aeons knew the Father through harmony and participation.
But Sophia desired something more.
She wished to know the Invisible Spirit directly.
Not through reflection.
Not through mediation.
Not through the ordered procession of the Aeons.
She desired immediate knowledge.
To gaze into the unfathomable depths from which all existence flowed.
At first this desire appeared noble.
What greater longing could Wisdom possess than the desire to know the Source?
Yet hidden within her yearning was impatience.
The subtle belief that she could reach the Infinite through her own power alone.
Without her divine counterpart.
Without the consent of the Fullness.
Without waiting for the proper order of emanation.
Thus Sophia conceived a thought.
And from that thought she sought to bring forth a being by herself.
The Pleroma trembled.
For never before had an Aeon acted alone.
The harmony that sustained the Fullness wavered.
A shadow appeared where no shadow had ever existed.
Sophia's creation emerged.
Not radiant.
Not balanced.
Not complete.
Something twisted.
Something malformed.
Something severed from the knowledge of its source.
Sophia beheld what she had brought forth and was overcome with horror.
For the first time, Wisdom encountered the consequence of acting apart from the Fullness.
The child she had produced possessed power but lacked understanding.
Life but not truth.
Will but not wisdom.
Ashamed, Sophia wrapped the being within a cloud and concealed it beyond the boundaries of the Pleroma.
Far from the sight of the Aeons.
Far from the light of the Father.
There it drifted alone.
Unseen.
Unnamed.
Forgotten.
And from that exile would come the greatest tragedy in the history of creation.
For the being hidden by Sophia would one day awaken.
And when it opened its eyes, it would know nothing of the realms above.
Nothing of Barbelo.
Nothing of the Invisible Spirit.
Nothing of the Fullness from which it had unknowingly fallen.
It would look upon itself.
See only itself.
And declare:
"I am God.
And there is no other."
Thus ended the age of perfect harmony.
And thus began the story of the worlds below.
